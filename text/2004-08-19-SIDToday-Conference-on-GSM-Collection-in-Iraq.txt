And the forecast for the future promises to be<br>an even greater challenge for both tactical and national SIGINT collectors and analysts as these<br>networks grow and multiply.<br>(S//SI) In response, the Strategy Division of CSRC (Collection Strategies and Requirements<br>Center) hosted the National-Tactical Iraqi GSM Collection Conference on 4-5 August 2004.<br>The purpose of the conference was to consider options relating to the improved integration of<br>collection efforts throughout the SIGINT system against Iraq's developing GSM networks and to<br>increase the capability to provide actionable intelligence to Coalition Forces in Iraq.<br>(S//SI) More than 70 analysts, linguists, and collectors from throughout the U.S. Intelligence<br>and Military communities, as well as representatives from GCHQ, participated in the two-day<br>conference. Following briefings on Iraq's GSM infrastructure, national and tactical collection<br>efforts, and geo-location strategies, conference participants discussed a number of Iraqi GSM<br>issues including signals development responsibilities, leveraging the unique capabilities of the<br>Cryptologic Support Teams (CSTs) deploying to Iraq, and improving national support to tactical<br>collection and development efforts. Along with building relationships and identifying<br>responsibilities with our community partners, a number of outstanding issues were assigned for<br>action at the conference's conclusion, ensuring the momentum started at the conference would<br>continue.<br>(U//FOUO) To view the notes, briefings, conference action paper, and attendee roster, visit<br>.
