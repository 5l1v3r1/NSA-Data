<p data-reactid=".ti.1.0.0.1.0.1.$0.0" class="SidTodayFilesDetailViewer-pages-page-paragraph">DYNAMIC PAGE -- HIGHEST POSSIBLE CLASSIFICATION IS<br>TOP SECRET // SI / TK // REL TO USA AUS CAN GBR NZL <br><br>(S//SI) SID TDY to USSOUTHCOM -- Focus on Haiti<br>FROM:<br>SOUTHCOM Account Manager (S112)<br>Run Date: 04/09/2004<br>FROM: <br>SOUTHCOM Account Manager (S112) <br>(S//SI) On 12 March 2004, <br> SID's Deputy Director for Customer Relationships<br>(DDCR), led a SID TDY to the U.S. Southern Command (USSOUTHCOM) to inform them about<br>SIGINT support for the Haiti crisis, and discuss their top concerns. The TDY was successful on<br>both counts and is just the beginning of significantly ramped up SIGINT attention for Haiti,<br>especially now with thousands of U.S. and multinational interim forces (MIF-H) in country.<br>(S//SI) In addition to the DDCR, the team included the Deputy Chief Customer Gateway, Latin<br>America and Caribbean Issue Managers, Regional Targets staff, Marine Corps Liaison Officer,<br>Requirements Analysis Center personnel, and the SID USSOUTHCOM Account Manager. MRSOC's<br>Executive Director was also present.<br>(S//SI) As USSOUTHCOM continues to evolve its mission and plans for Operation SECURE<br>TOMORROW in Haiti, senior personnel listed for us several priorities :<br>need for SIGINT support for tactical operations<br>illegal armed groups' planning, organizing, and directing attacks against MIF-H<br>sources of funding for these groups<br>identification of Haitians who are "good, bad, unknown" actors for follow-on action<br>former president Aristide's involvement in Haitian affairs<br>The Requirements Analysis Center (RAC, S16) determined that the results of this Needs<br>Assessment are already covered in the current USSOUTHCOM Information Needs; however, this<br>list will help focus the SIGINT system on what matters most now. We asked USSOUTHCOM to<br>regularly send us Key Reads.<br>(TS//SI) The USSOUTHCOM J2 gave high praise to strategic-level SIGINT reporting to date. He<br>then stressed his immediate need for tactical level reporting to support the troops on the<br>ground. Our team described actions already taken , including:<br>deploying a NIST<br>partnering with the Canadians on collection, analysis and reporting<br>redirecting an MRSOC analyst to CSG SOUTHCOM<br>clearing the way for tactical SECRET level linguists to work traffic,<br>performing call chaining analysis on High Powered Cordless Phones,<br>enhancing our linguistic pool<br>issuing reporting guidance on product release to MIF-H<br>The J2 remains engaged and interested in the progress on all of these fronts. While there, we<br>also attended the first USSOUTHCOM-hosted Intelligence Community-wide VTC on Haiti currently being held twice per week.<br>(S//SI) NSA is committed to SIGINT support for SECURE TOMORROW. We will achieve success<br>through continued dialogue to understand our customers' prioritized needs, being transparent<br>with them about our capabilities, plans, and limitations, and quickly taking action that will result<br>in USSOUTHCOM's desired end results.<br>(S//SI) USSOUTHCOM's Crisis Page for Haiti can be found on Intelink.<br><br> </p><p data-reactid=".ti.1.0.0.1.0.1.$1.0" class="SidTodayFilesDetailViewer-pages-page-paragraph">"(U//FOUO) SIDtoday articles may not be republished or reposted outside NSANet<br>without the consent of S0121 (DL sid_comms)."<br><br>DYNAMIC PAGE -- HIGHEST POSSIBLE CLASSIFICATION IS<br>TOP SECRET // SI / TK // REL TO USA AUS CAN GBR NZL<br>DERIVED FROM: NSA/CSSM 1-52, DATED 08 JAN 2007 DECLASSIFY ON: 20320108 <br><br> </p>
