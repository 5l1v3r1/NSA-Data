)<br>or the DIRNSA to the various committees ( HPSCI, SSCI, HAC and SAC and any other<br>committees having jurisdiction over the subject of the notification) informing them of a<br>"significant Central<br>Intelligence and the heads of all...agencies...involved in intelligence activities shall keep the<br>congressional intelligence committees fully and currently informed of all intelligence activities...<br>including any significant anticipated intelligence activity and any significant intelligence failure".<br>To fulfill our obligation to keep Congress "fully and currently informed", NSA has implemented<br>NSA/CSS Regulation No. 010-01, Congressional Notification Procedures .<br>(U) What constitutes a "significant activity"?<br>(U) A proposed activity which meets one or more of the following criteria may constitute a<br>significant activity:<br>Activity raises or will have a major impact on foreign policy or national security concerns;<br>Affects significant intelligence resources (e.g. major cost overrun, termination of<br>contract);<br>Entails significant risk of exposure of comprise of classified information or intelligence<br>sources or methods, which would likely result in loss of human life;<br>Presents a reasonably foreseeable risk of a break of diplomatic relations, or a serious<br>deterioration in relations between the U.S. and one or more of its allies;<br>Activity has been directly approved by the President or the National Security Council;<br>Involves a proposal to authorize the export of "controlled equipment" under particularly<br>sensitive circumstances.<br>(U) What constitutes a "significant intelligence failure"?<br>(U) An activity which meets one or more of the following criteria may constitute a significant<br>intelligence failure:<br>Total or pervasive failure or compromise of a technical collection system previously<br>believed to be reliable and secure is discovered;<br>Pervasive failure to protect, or an unanticipated or thus far undetected compromise of, a<br>category of intelligence sources and/or methods is discovered;<br>Losses of intelligence sources and methods are incurred on such a scale, or over such an<br>extended period of time, that the possibility of a systematic compromise is indicated;<br>Unanticipated loss of life has occurred in the conduct of an intelligence activity, even<br>when the intelligence activity itself was successful;<br>Discovery of intelligence activities by foreign partners which have caused, or are likely to<br>cause, serious interruption or other damage to the conduct of U.S. foreign intelligence or<br><br> </p><p data-reactid=".ti.1.0.0.1.0.1.$1.0" class="SidTodayFilesDetailViewer-pages-page-paragraph">counterintelligence
